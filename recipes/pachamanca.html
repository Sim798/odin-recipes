Pachamanca

Pachamanca: an Incas's traditional dish still alive

What is Pachamanca?

Pachamanca is a traditional Peruvian dish inherited from Incas. It represents the strong bond that the Incas had with Mother Earth.

Ingredients

  • 2 legs of Lamb
  • 4 Chickens
  • 20 Pork Ribs
  • 6 cuy
  • 20 sweet potatoes
  • 20 Oca Potatoes
  • 20 potatoes
  • Broad Beans
  • 10 Corn Cobs split in half
  • Aji panca
  • Pepper
  • 10 Plantains
  • Salt
  • 1 kg Paria Cheese
  • Plantain leaves

Preparation

  1. Make a hole in the ground where you want to prepare the Pachamanca
  2. Light a fire near where the Pachamanca is going to be prepared and heat a large amount of stones
  3. Once the stones are very hot, place a bed of these carefully in the bottom of the hole with the help of tweezers. On top of them, place the food in layers:
    1. The sweet potatoes, potatoes, oca potatoes, and then some hot stones on top
    2. On the second layer, place the meats seasoned with salt, pepper, garlic, and aj√≠ panca to taste
    3. Place most hot stones on top since the meats take longer to cook. All this is covered with plantain leaves to protect the food and avoid contact with soil.
  4. On top, place the beans, cuy, portions of pariah cheese, bananas, and corn cobs and cover again with plantain leaves and a piece of jute.
  5. Cover the whole thing with a poncho or thick cloth to keep it warm
